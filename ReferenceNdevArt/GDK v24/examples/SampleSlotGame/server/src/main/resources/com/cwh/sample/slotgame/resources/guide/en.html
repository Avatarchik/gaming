ULTIMATE TEXAS HOLD'EM

Ultimate Texas Hold'em is a community card poker game. The object is to make a better five-card poker hand than the dealer, using your two-card hand and five community cards. There is also an optional Trips bet, where you win if your final five-card hand is Three of a Kind or higher.

Regular Play

The game uses a standard 52-card deck, shuffled after every round.

Click on the chip stack located in the bottom right corner of the screen to select the desired bet amount. For convenience, the value of the last chip clicked on the chip stack is held in memory and can be repeated without returning to the chip stack. The chip stack only appears when it's possible to change denominations.

Higher credit denominations: 1K=1000, 1M=1 000 000, 1B=1 000 000 000.

You must make equal bets on both the Ante and Blind. When you place a bet on the Ante bet area, an identical bet is placed on the Blind bet area, and vice versa. You can also place an optional Trips bet by clicking in the Trips bet area. The Trips bet can be for a different amount than the Ante/Blind bets.

Click the left panel or the table card to show the table limits and payouts.

To remove a bet, hold down the Shift key and click on a chip to remove it from the table. The Undo button will undo your last action, whether it was adding or removing a chip.

Click Deal. After the first hand, you can use the Same Bet button to repeat your last bet(s) and start the deal.

The cards are dealt and your hole cards are turned face up.

At this point, you can bet or check. You can make a Play bet of either 3 or 4 times your Ante bet, e.g., if you bet 5.00 on the Ante and selected 3x, the Play bet would be 15.00. All the cards are then turned over and a winner determined with no additional betting rounds.

If you select Check instead, you can continue without increasing your bet. The first 3 community cards (known as the flop) are turned over. You can then check again to see the rest of the community cards without increasing your bet, or you can increase your bet by 2 times your Ante bet. If you make the 2x bet, the final two community cards will be turned over and a winner determined without an additional round of betting.

If you checked again, the final two community cards are turned over and then you can either select Fold and lose your Ante and Blind bets, or you can make a bet equal to the Ante bet. The dealer's cards will be turned over and a winner determined.

Play bets do not apply to the Trips bet, which is never increased after the intial bet.

Results

The system determines whether you or the dealer has the best five-card poker hand, using any combination of hole cards and community cards. The cards in the winning five-card hand will be raised slightly from the table.

The individual Ante, Blind, Play and Trips bets all have different conditions under which they pay out:

Dealer Qualifying

The dealer needs at least a pair to qualify. However, unlike some other casino games, you don't automatically win if the dealer does not qualify.

Ante Bet

If the dealer qualifies and you have the higher hand then the Ante will pay even money (1:1). If the dealer qualifies and the dealer has the higher hand then your Ante bet will lose. If it's a tie or the dealer does not qualify then the Ante bet will push (your bet is returned to you).

Blind Bet

If your hand wins with a straight or better, the Blind bet will pay according to the pay table. If you have a better hand than the dealer, but less than a straight, the Blind bet pushes. If the dealer has the higher hand, your Blind bet loses, even if the dealer did not qualify.

Play Bet

If you have the higher hand the Play bet will pay even money. If the dealer has the higher hand the Play bet will lose, regardless of whether or not the dealer has qualified.

Ties

If you and dealer tie, then the Ante, Blind, and Play bets will all push.

Trips Bet Results

You win if your final 5-card hand is Trips (3 of a Kind) or higher. Winning Trips bets are paid out regardless of whether the main hand wins, loses or pushes. You can win a Trips bet even if you fold.

For the exact payouts, see the Table Limits and Payouts panel.

Hand Rankings

Hands are ranked using the following standard poker rankings.

Abbreviations: c=clubs, d=diamonds, h=hearts, s=spades. Suits are not used to break ties.

Hand Rankings Hand Descriptions
Royal Flush

An Ace, King, Queen, Jack and Ten in the same suit. E.g., As-Ks-Qs-Js-10s.
Straight Flush

Five cards in sequence of the same suit. E.g., 6h-7h-8h-9h-10h. The Ace can play either high or low, i.e., the lowest Straight Flush is 5c-4c-3c-2c-Ac.
Quads (Four of a Kind) Four cards of the same rank, accompanied by a kicker (an unmatched card). E.g., Kd-Kc-Kh-Ks-2d. Ranked by the four matched cards, so 5-5-5-5-3 would beat 4-4-4-4-K.
Full House

Three of a Kind combined with a Pair. E.g., Ad-Ac-Ah-7s-7h. Ranked by the Three of a Kind, so 4-4-4-2-2 would beat 3-3-3-A-A
Flush

Any five cards of the same suit, but not in sequence. Ranked by the top card, and then by the next card, so that Qc-10c-9c-5c-2c beats Qs-10s-8s-7s-6s.

Straight

Five cards in sequence, but of different suits. E.g., 6d-7c-8d-9s-10h. The Ace can be either high or low, e.g., A-K-Q-J-10 and 5-4-3-2-A. Ranked by the top card. Identical Straights will push, i.e., the other cards not used in the Straight are not considered.

Trips (Three of a Kind)

Three cards of the same rank and and two unrelated kickers. E.g., Jd-Jc-Jh-5s-4h. Ranked by the three matched cards.
Two Pair

Two different sets of pairs and a kicker. The highest pair has priority when comparing hands. If the high pairs are the same, then the low pairs are compared, and then the remaining card.
Pair

Two cards of the same rank, accompanied by three kickers of different ranks, such as Kd-Ks-3h-7d-5s. Ranked by the pair, followed by each kicker in turn, so A-A-K-5-3 would beat A-A-K-5-2.
High Card

If none of the above combinations can be made, the hand with the highest card wins, with kickers determining the winner in ties.

Game Options

Go to Main Menu - Options to bring up the following menu:

Sound Effects: This turns the game sounds on and off.

Dealer Voice: This will turn the messages such as "Player wins" on and off.

Ambient Sound: This will turn on the sounds of a normal casino, such as people talking in the background. (These sounds are very quiet compared to the other sounds.)

High Quality Graphics: You can uncheck this if you are experiencing slowdowns in play because of your computer's processing abilities. Note: Slowdowns in play can also be the result of Internet-related issues such as peak usage periods.

Full Animations: You can uncheck this to have the cards just appear in the final position without showing the animations.

Fit To Window: Check this to stretch the game to match the size of the window.
